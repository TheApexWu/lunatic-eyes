Research: Attention Metrics in HCI

The K-coefficient, introduced by Krejtz et al. (2016), provides a quantitative measure of the transition between ambient and focal attention modes. By combining fixation duration with saccade amplitude, the coefficient captures whether a viewer is scanning broadly or processing deeply.

Notes

Ambient attention: short fixations, long saccades. Focal attention: long fixations, short saccades. The transition between these modes is what distinguishes active reading from passive scrolling.

Related Work

Duchowski et al. proposed using entropy-based metrics derived from gaze transitions as a measure of cognitive load. Higher entropy correlates with exploration; lower entropy correlates with focused processing.
